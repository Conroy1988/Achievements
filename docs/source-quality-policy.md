# Source Quality Policy

This project distinguishes GitHub-authored primary evidence, independently reproduced behaviour, and community-reported thresholds.

## Evidence levels

1. **Official** — documented by GitHub in maintained product documentation, GitHub-owned product publications or changelogs, or preserved first-party documentation for a completed historical programme.
2. **Confirmed** — independently reproduced under recorded conditions with dates, attribution controls, and adequate counterexample handling.
3. **Observed** — supported by credible public observations but not yet independently reproduced across the required conditions.
4. **Community-reported** — consistently or widely reported without sufficient primary or independent evidence.
5. **Unknown** — unsupported, materially contradictory, or too incomplete for a reliable claim.

## Historical sources

A preserved GitHub documentation page can establish what GitHub officially stated about a completed historical programme. It must be labelled as archived, cited with its capture date, and must not be used to infer current mutable platform behaviour.

## Citation requirements

Every numerical threshold or behavioural claim must include a primary source, reproduction note, or explicit uncertainty label. Screenshots must include a date and redact private information.

## Maintenance

Achievement behaviour may change without notice. Contributors must re-check time-sensitive claims before updating a guide and record the verification date.
