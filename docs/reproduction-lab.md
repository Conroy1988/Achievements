## Achievement reproduction laboratory

The laboratory converts high-priority uncertainty into controlled, reproducible tests. It does **not** authorise spam, fabricated activity, false attribution, weakened production safeguards, or unnecessary collection of personal data.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> A successful test does not automatically change an evidence classification. Independent reproduction, contradictory results, processing delays, and methodological limitations still require human review.

## Laboratory controls

Every protocol defines:

- a bounded research question;
- ethical and privacy constraints;
- prerequisites and controlled variables;
- exact observations to record;
- stop conditions;
- negative-result handling; and
- linked claim and research-task identifiers.

## How to participate

1. Select an open task from the [research hub](research-hub.md).
2. Read the complete protocol in [`api/lab-protocols.json`](../api/lab-protocols.json).
3. Use legitimate activity that would exist without the research project.
4. Record positive, negative, and inconclusive results.
5. Submit the observation through the [structured evidence form](https://github.com/Conroy1988/Achievements/issues/new?template=evidence-observation.yml).
6. Allow automated privacy screening and human review to complete before any evidence classification changes.

## Non-negotiable exclusions

- No credentials, tokens, private repository URLs, private communications, payment details, billing information, or unnecessary identifiers.
- No meaningless pull requests, repeated issue churn, false co-author trailers, coordinated fake sponsorships, or pressure on maintainers to mark answers accepted.
- No bypassing branch protection, required review, or organisational safeguards.
- No claim that a missing public signal proves an achievement was not earned.
