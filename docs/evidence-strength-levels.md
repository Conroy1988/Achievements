# Evidence strength levels

Use consistent evidence grades when documenting GitHub achievement behaviour.

## Official

Directly stated by GitHub in maintained documentation, a GitHub-owned product publication, or a preserved first-party document that records a completed historical programme. Archived material must be identified as historical and must not be presented as a current mutable contract.

## Confirmed

Independently reproduced under recorded conditions with sufficient dated evidence and attribution controls.

## Observed

Seen in credible public examples but not yet independently reproduced across the required conditions.

## Community reported

Reported by users or third-party references without sufficient direct verification.

## Unknown

Plausible but unsupported or materially contradicted. Do not present as fact.

Always record the verification date, preserve limitations, and link the strongest available source.
