## Evidence register policy

The public evidence register makes the basis for achievement claims inspectable without publishing credentials, private repository data, billing information, personal correspondence, or unnecessary personal identifiers.

## Publication requirements

Every evidence record must:

1. identify one canonical achievement;
2. state one reviewable claim;
3. use the project's shared evidence vocabulary;
4. include observation and submission dates;
5. identify its reproduction state and reviewer decision;
6. link to the relevant guide and section;
7. record contradictory evidence when known; and
8. declare a privacy status.

## Privacy controls

The register must not contain:

- access tokens, cookies, credentials, or private URLs;
- sponsorship amounts, payment references, or billing details;
- unredacted email addresses, real names, or account identifiers that are not necessary to evaluate a public claim;
- screenshots containing private repository names, private discussions, direct messages, or analytics; or
- evidence obtained through impersonation, spam, fabricated activity, or deceptive engagement.

A record may refer to a redacted external artifact, but the public JSON must contain only the privacy-safe description and approved public URL.

## Reviewer decisions

- **Accepted** — sufficient evidence supports the stated claim.
- **Accepted with limitations** — the core claim is usable, but important boundaries remain undocumented or unreproduced.
- **Provisional** — plausible evidence exists, but independent reproduction or stronger sourcing is required.
- **Rejected** — the submitted evidence does not support the claim or violates project policy.
- **Needs review** — triage is incomplete.

## Corrections and contradictions

Contradictory evidence is retained rather than silently removed. A later review may change the evidence level or reviewer decision, but the correction must be traceable through repository history.

## Source preservation

When a public source is unstable, add an archive URL where lawful and technically available. Archived material supplements the original source; it does not automatically increase the evidence classification.

## Relationship to guides

The register provides traceability. The achievement guides remain the human-readable interpretation of the evidence, including caveats, practical meaning, and current maintenance status.
