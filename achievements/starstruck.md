## Starstruck

**Status:** Earnable  
**Tiered:** Yes  
**Evidence level:** Strong community consensus; GitHub does not publish the complete threshold table in its general profile documentation.

## Trigger

Starstruck recognises repositories you created that reach community-reported star thresholds.

## Community-reported tiers

| Level | Repository stars |
|---|---:|
| Base | 16 |
| Bronze | 128 |
| Silver | 512 |
| Gold | 4,096 |

These values are widely reproduced but should be treated as observed platform behaviour rather than a formal guarantee.

## What appears to matter

- The repository must be associated with your authorship or creation.
- Stars must be genuine GitHub user actions.
- The relevant threshold must be reached on a qualifying repository.
- The achievement can update after the threshold event rather than instantly.

## Legitimate growth strategy

Build something useful, explain it clearly, include screenshots or examples, add an appropriate licence, respond to issues, and share it in communities where it is genuinely relevant.

## Prohibited or unreliable approaches

- Buying stars.
- Coordinated star exchanges.
- Automated or fake accounts.
- Misleading promotion.

Artificial engagement damages repository credibility and may violate platform rules.

## Troubleshooting

Confirm the public star count, repository ownership history, and achievement visibility settings. Keep dated evidence because star counts can later fall below a threshold.
